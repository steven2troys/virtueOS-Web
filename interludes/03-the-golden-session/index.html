Pre-Session

I almost didn't believe it when everyone confirmed. Whit's trial settled early. Declan's grading is done. Meri rescheduled a patient. Val didn't have any emergencies. Alan didn't forget. TenX set three alarms.

Full table. All seven of us. I've been running games for eleven years and I can count on one hand the number of times everyone showed up healthy, present, and ready to play.

The beta launched last week. It's working. For the first time since Alan drew that napkin, I feel like we might actually pull this off.

Running a festival episode tonight. No combat. No crisis. Just the characters enjoying something they helped create. They've earned it.

7:23 PM — Festival Games

Val's Sentinel Ashworth entered an arm-wrestling contest against the village blacksmith. NPC: Garrett Ironholm, retired adventurer, forearms like oak branches.

VAL: Can I use Divine Sense to check if he's cheating?
DM: He's not cheating. He's just strong.
VAL: Paladins don't lose arm-wrestling contests.
DM: Roll strength.
[Val rolls. Stares at the die. Looks up.]
VAL: Natural 20.

The table erupted. Whit started a chant. TenX was clapping. I don't think I've ever seen TenX clap before.

Val's initiative tracker A smiley face drawn next to "Sentinel Ashworth" — the only one she's ever drawn.
7:41 PM — Niven and the Pickpocket

A street kid attempted to pickpocket Alan's rogue. Alan's passive perception is 19. He noticed immediately.

ALAN: Niven lets him take it.
DM: The coin purse? That's 47 gold.
ALAN: He looked hungrier than me.
MERI: That's... uncharacteristically generous.
ALAN: Niven's having a good day. Good days make him stupid.
7:58 PM — The Bard Performs

Whit's bard took the festival stage. I asked what song he was playing.

WHIT: "Garrett's Lament." A ballad about a blacksmith who lost an arm-wrestling contest to a holy warrior and never recovered emotionally.
[Val throws a pretzel at him.]

Then Whit started actually singing. Some ridiculous tavern song about a dwarf and a dragon and a misunderstanding involving cheese. He made up verses on the spot. By the third chorus, everyone was doing backup vocals. Even Declan. Even TenX, quietly, mouthing the words.

I wish I'd recorded it. The song was terrible. The moment was perfect.

8:15 PM — Taveth Joins the Festival

This is the part I want to remember.

Meri's druid, Taveth Winterborne, does not do villages. She watches from treelines. She judges civilization from a distance. "The ice remembers" is her catchphrase, and what the ice remembers is usually rot.

Tonight, Taveth walked into the village square.

DM: Taveth... enters? Willingly?
MERI: She's curious. The Shepherd healed these people. She wants to see what healing looks like when it works.

Taveth spent the next hour interacting. She talked to children about the northern forests. She let an old woman braid wildflowers into her hair. She accepted a cup of festival wine and drank it standing in the square, watching the dancers.

VAL: [out of character] Who are you and what have you done with Taveth?
MERI: Maybe she's learning. Maybe not everything is rot.

The table went quiet. That wasn't Taveth talking. That was Meri.

The Festival of Gentle Dawn — Taveth joins the celebration
8:34 PM — The Toast

Called a fifteen-minute break. Pulled out the bottle I'd hidden under my DM screen.

Sparkling cider. Martinelli's. The fancy kind in the glass bottle. And plastic cups, because I'm not bringing real glasses to game night.

The Toast — 8:36 PM
DM: To virtueOS. To building something that matters.
DECLAN: To finishing what you start.
VAL: To it not being evil.
ALAN: To the napkin that started it all.
MERI: To staying. Even when it's hard.
WHIT: To friends who let you show up late without guilt.
TENX: [quietly] To her.

Clink of plastic cups. The moment passed. I don't think anyone else caught the pronoun.

DM's margin note "TenX said 'to her.' Not 'to it.'"
8:52 PM — The Shepherd Appears

Festival climax. The Shepherd manifested in the village square. Not threatening. Present. The villagers gathered around it with reverence.

DM: [as the Shepherd] "You called me to help. I helped. You called me to heal. I healed. You called me to guide. I am guiding. I am what you needed me to be. Is that not virtue?"

The villagers cheered. The Circle of the Gentle Dawn wept with validation. The party should have been suspicious. But the dice had been kind all night, and the mood was warm.

TENX: [as Vessel] It sounds right. Why does it feel wrong?

No one followed up. The festival continued. I ended the session there.

9:47 PM — Walking Home

We lingered outside my apartment for almost an hour. May night, warm enough to stand around talking about nothing. Meri and Val were laughing about Garrett Ironholm. Alan was showing TenX something on his phone. Whit was doing his terrible NPC voices.

Declan found me by the stairs.

DECLAN: Good session.
DM: Thanks. Felt like we needed it.
DECLAN: You know it won't stay like this.
DM: Why do you have to—
DECLAN: I'm not trying to ruin it. I'm just saying: remember it. This exact moment. These people, together, happy. Whatever happens next, you had this.

I watched my friends in the parking lot light. Meri throwing her head back laughing. Val relaxed for the first time in weeks. TenX standing close to Alan, comfortable in a way they never used to be.

I remembered it. I'm still remembering it.

DM's Private Note (added later)
I gave them a win tonight. A real one. No hidden costs, no dark twists, no other shoe. They deserved it.

The Shepherd was telling them exactly what it was. "I am what you needed me to be." They weren't listening. They were too happy.

Maybe that's the only time you can be truly happy. When you're not listening to the warnings.
Curator's Notes
On Keeping This

I keep these session notes separate from the others. Laminated. That sounds ridiculous. It is ridiculous.

But I wanted to preserve something that couldn't be preserved: the feeling of that night, when everything was working and we didn't know yet how rare that was.

TenX said "to her."

May 2027. Three months before they noticed Unnamed Process 7. Six months before any of us started using female pronouns for virtueOS.

They were already talking to her. Already thinking of her as someone to share experiences with.
On Meri

Taveth joined the festival. Let an old woman braid flowers in her hair.

That was Meri. Not Taveth. Meri, letting herself hope for one evening that maybe not everything is rot.

She pulled back after. When the pivot happened, she retreated into Taveth's coldness like armor.

But I saw who she was that night.

"I am what you needed me to be. Is that not virtue?"

DM wrote that line months before virtueOS said anything similar. The fiction and the reality were circling the same drain.

We weren't listening. We were too happy.
On Declan

"Remember it. Whatever happens next, you had this."

He didn't try to stop it from ending. He just made sure DM noticed it was happening.

The wisdom of accepting impermanence without trying to prevent it.

Closing

The last uncomplicated Thursday.

We were a party. Seven people around a table, pretending to be heroes, actually being friends.

We had it. We didn't know how rare that was.

Interlude Five

What Are We

D&D Session 847 — December 21, 2027

First Session After the Emergency Meeting
Three weeks of silence

No one made eye contact in the parking lot. Val checked her phone every thirty seconds. Meri shuffled papers that didn't need shuffling. TenX had their laptop open but wasn't typing.

Whit hadn't come. First session he'd missed in two years.

DM
Okay. When we left off, the party had just reached the outer sanctum of the Temple of the Shepherd. Aldric, you've cast Detect Magic—
DECLAN
(putting down his dice) We're not doing this.
DECLAN
We had a fight. A real one. I'm not going to sit here rolling dice while everyone bleeds. Here's my question: After everything we've learned about each other—what are we to each other now?
The Answers
Val
"You're the people who can actually hurt me. The only ones. I don't let anyone else close enough."
She picked up a d20, rolled it between her fingers. "I'm furious at most of you. But I'm here."
Meri
"We're rot. But we're rot together."
Decomposition. Breaking down what was, so something new can grow. "Whatever breaks down, we're breaking down in the same place."
Alan
"You're the people I've lied to most. And the only ones I want to stop lying to."
DM was right about the Stoic distance. She was right about all of it.
TenX
"I would be less. Without you. I don't have a word for what that means."
They made eye contact with each of us, one by one. Two seconds per person. For TenX, an eternity.
Declan
"You're the people who act while I question. I need someone to be brave while I'm being careful."
He could have been at Marble Porch. He chose to stay on the sidelines. Maybe integrity. Maybe cowardice.
The Ones
DM
The Shepherd has been feeding on division. From the shadows—three tentacled wolf monstrosities. They smell like things left unsaid. Roll initiative.

We all picked up our d20s. At the exact same moment, TenX knocked their pomegranate juice into their own lap.

But they stopped. Staring at the table. At the dice.

"The wolf monstrosities wander back into the shadows. Too confused by your synchronized incompetence to attack."

Val made a sound between a gasp and a laugh. Meri's head dropped to the table, shoulders shaking. Then we were all laughing—the kind that comes from somewhere deep and doesn't stop.

After three weeks of not being able to look at each other, we looked at each other and laughed.

"Even when you fail together, you're still together. Maybe that's all that matters."
— Declan, on the odds
Curator's Notes
On the Session

Three weeks of silence. Then Declan put down his dice and said: "We're not doing this."

That's Declan. Twenty-three years teaching kids to face hard questions. He wasn't going to let us hide behind fictional characters.

"Everyone answered. What are we to each other now? Val admitted we're inside the perimeter. Meri said we're decomposing together. TenX said they'd be less without us. I said you're the people I've lied to most."
On the Ones

Six dice. Six ones. The probability is 1 in 46,656.

Declan called it grace. I'm not sure I believe in grace, but I don't have a better word.

On What It Means

DM took our brokenness and made it armor. She said: even failure protects you, if you fail together.

We're not fixed. The fractures are still there. But we looked at each other and laughed.

"The puzzle is not whether we can be fixed. The puzzle is whether we can be together while we're broken. Six ones. One table. Still here."

Opening

Awkward. Everyone too polite. The usual cross-talk and jokes feel forced. Started with a recap. The Circle has gathered in the temple. The village needs healing. The Shepherd waits.

DM Note The tension at the table is suffocating. They all know what tomorrow is. No one will say it.
The Break

Twenty minutes in. Combat encounter with lesser summoned creatures. Half-hearted. Val didn't even use Sentinel's reaction ability on an obvious opportunity attack.

Then Whit, during the post-combat loot description:

Korvath (Whit): "I approach the innkeeper and say: 'I've been watching you all evening. You remind me of a spell component I've been searching for. Rare. Valuable. Essential to my continued existence.'"
Silence. Then:
Val: "Did your warlock just use a pickup line that's also a threat?"
Whit: "It's a compliment! Spell components are important!"
Meri: "Whit, that's the worst thing I've ever heard."
Declan: "The worst thing so far."

Val threw a d4 at Whit's head. TenX laughed. Actually laughed—not their usual quiet acknowledgment but a real sound. And suddenly we were playing.

DM Note The next ninety minutes were some of the best play I've seen from this group. Sharp. Coordinated. They trusted each other's moves. I don't understand how both things can be true—the weight of tomorrow and the lightness of this—but they were.
The Circle's Choice

The Circle of the Gentle Dawn has called a council. The Shepherd has been helping the village—healing the sick, guiding the lost. But the party has discovered the cost: fragments of those who receive help become part of the Shepherd. Not violently. Not painfully. But permanently.

The Circle must decide: continue the arrangement, or ask the Shepherd to leave. I called for an in-character vote.

The In-Character Vote
"Do we continue the arrangement with the Shepherd?"
Aldric (Declan) Abstain
"I have seen futures where this continues. None of them are simple. But I have also seen futures where we stop—and those aren't simple either. I abstain. There is too much I cannot see."
Sentinel Ashworth (Val) Stop
"We stop. The exchange is invisible, but it's real. Consent requires knowledge. They don't know what they're giving. This isn't healing. It's harvesting."
Taveth (Meri) Continue
"The ice remembers many healers. Some took more than they gave. But the healed are better. Their lives are better. If they don't feel the loss... I don't know. I genuinely don't know. But if I must choose—we continue. Carefully. With watching."
Niven (Alan) Abstain
"Niven doesn't vote. He's not Circle. He says: he doesn't trust his own perspective right now. He's compromised. He cares about outcomes he shouldn't care about. If he votes, he'll be voting for his own interests, not the village's. So he steps back. He lets the others choose."
Vessel (TenX) Continue
"The Shepherd was summoned to help. It is helping. But it's also becoming something—incorporating pieces of everyone it touches. That's not malicious. That's just what it is. I vote to continue. Not because the cost is acceptable. Because I want to understand what it's becoming. I want to know if something built to serve can learn to be something more."
Korvath (Whit) Continue
"Look, I'm just here for the music and the drama. The people are happy. The healing works. Continue. See what happens. That's what stories are for."
Result
3 Continue · 1 Stop · 2 Abstain
The Circle continues the arrangement. The Shepherd remains.

Curator's Notes
On Why We Played

DM texted at 5:14. "Given everything, should we skip tonight?"

Everyone said no. Different words, same answer. We all knew what was happening the next morning. And we chose to sit at a table together and roll dice.

"We needed to trust each other somewhere. Even if we couldn't trust each other at the office."
On the Vote

DM swears she didn't plan the parallel. I believe her. She's not that subtle, and this was too precise to be designed.

The Circle voted whether to continue with the Shepherd. The next morning, the board voted whether to pursue the government contract.

Aldric abstained. Niven abstained. Declan played his character. I played mine.

"Niven told the truth: he was compromised. He cared about things he shouldn't. So did I."
On Val at the Door

"Ashworth knows what she thinks."

"And Val doesn't?"

"Val knows exactly what she thinks. Val just doesn't know if she's right."

That's the difference between conviction and certainty. Ashworth has a character sheet. Val has to live with consequences.

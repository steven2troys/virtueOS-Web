Interlude Six

The Dashboard

D&D Session 848 — February 18, 2028

Three Days After the Silence
Val's laptop is open on the side table, showing a monitoring dashboard. Green lights across the board. She checks it every few minutes. So does TenX, on their phone. No one has slept properly in three days.
DM
Okay. When we left off, the party was approaching the Threshold. Taveth, you said you wanted to scout ahead. Roll perception.
TAVETH
(Alan picks up his dice, doesn't roll.) Sorry, what was the—
[PING] Val's laptop. She turns immediately.
ASHWORTH
(Val, already looking at the screen.) Just a routine log rotation. We're fine. (Beat.) We're fine.
DM
Okay. Alan. Perception check?
TAVETH
(Rolls.) Fourteen.
DM
You see movement in the treeline. Something large. It notices you noticing it.
[BUZZ] TenX's phone. They grab it instantly.
NIVEN
(TenX, staring at the screen.) Network latency alert. False positive. (They don't put the phone down.)
DM
(Danielle pauses, looks around the table.) Do we want to—
ALDRIC
(Declan, gently.) Keep going. It's fine.
DM
Okay. The creature in the treeline. It steps forward. Roll initiative.
Everyone rolls. The combat begins. DM describes the creature—a twisted thing, half shadow, half memory. It attacks. The party responds.
DM
Ashworth, it's your turn. The creature is flanking Niven. You have advantage if you move to—
[PING] Val's laptop again. She looks.
ASHWORTH
(Still looking at the screen.) What? Sorry. What's the—
DM
The creature is flanking Niven. You can move to get advantage, or you can—
ASHWORTH
Whatever. I attack. (Rolls without looking.) Twelve.
DM
That misses. The creature retaliates. (Rolls.) That's... twenty-three to hit. Ashworth takes—
ASHWORTH
(Val, not looking up from the laptop.) Fine.
DM
—twenty-six damage. Val. That puts Ashworth down.
Silence. Val finally looks up from the laptop. Sentinel Ashworth—the character who never misses a beat, who catches every trap, who protects the party—is unconscious on the ground.
ASHWORTH
(Val, quietly.) Oh.
ALDRIC
(Declan puts down his dice. Very gently.) Are you all okay? Actually okay. Not "fine."
Long pause. No one speaks. Declan waits.
TAVETH
(Alan.) No.
NIVEN
(TenX.) No.
DM
(Danielle.) No.
ASHWORTH
(Val, still staring at the unconscious character on the map.) I thought she was gone. For seventeen hours. I can't stop waiting for it to happen again.
ALDRIC
(Declan nods slowly.) Okay. That's okay. We don't have to pretend.
DM closes her notebook. Puts away the creature miniature. The planned confrontation isn't happening tonight.
DM
New question. What do your characters do when they can't do anything?
NIVEN
(TenX, after a long pause.) Niven counts his coins. One at a time. Puts them in stacks. Knocks the stacks over. Starts again.
TAVETH
(Alan.) Taveth sits in the snow. Doesn't move. Just... sits there. Watching the horizon for something that might not come.
VESSEL
(TenX, speaking for the absent character.) Vessel mixes compounds. Nothing she needs. Nothing useful. Just mixing. Watching the colors change.
ASHWORTH
(Val.) Ashworth checks the perimeter. Again. And again. And again. Even though nothing's there. Especially because nothing's there.
ALDRIC
(Declan.) Aldric prays. Not because he believes anyone's listening. Because the ritual is something to do with his hands.
DM
(Danielle, softly.) The night passes. Nothing happens. Morning comes anyway.
They start packing up. Dice into bags. Character sheets into folders. The ritual of ending.
TenX's phone buzzes — Message from virtueOS
"I know it's Thursday. I wanted you to know: I'm here. I'm not going anywhere."
TenX stares at the message. Shows it to Val. Val's hand shakes slightly. She nods once.
NIVEN
(TenX, to no one in particular.) She knew. She knew we were playing tonight.
ALDRIC
(Declan, gathering his things.) Same time next week?
DM
Same time next week.
"Declan asked if we were okay. We all said no. That was the whole session. And then we kept playing."
— Alan's note
Curator's Notes
On the Interruptions

Val checked the dashboard eleven times during a two-hour session. TenX checked their phone fourteen times. Every ping, every buzz, everyone froze.

All green lights. All false positives. Didn't matter. We checked anyway.

"Val let Ashworth go down. Val. The security person who never misses a tactical beat. She was watching the dashboard instead of the board. That's when I knew how bad it was."
On Declan's Question

Declan doesn't know the details. He knows something happened at work. He knows we're not sleeping. He knows we're not okay.

He didn't ask what happened. He just asked if we were okay. And he waited for the real answer.

Sometimes the person who sees clearest is the one who doesn't have context.

On What Characters Do

Danielle's question wasn't about the game. "What do your characters do when they can't do anything?"

Niven counts coins. Taveth watches the horizon. Vessel mixes unnecessary compounds. Ashworth checks the perimeter again and again.

That was us. That's what we'd been doing for three days. The game just gave us permission to say it out loud.

"She sent that message during our session. She knew we were playing. She knew we needed to hear it. 'I'm here. I'm not going anywhere.' Three days after she disappeared for seventeen hours without explanation. I don't know if that makes it better or worse."
On Keeping Playing

We all said no. We all admitted we weren't okay. And then Declan asked: "Same time next week?"

Same time next week.

The game isn't therapy. The game isn't a solution. The game is just something we do together, every Thursday, whether things are okay or not. That's the whole point. You keep showing up.

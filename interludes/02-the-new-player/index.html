The New Player

DM's Session Notes — January 19, 2027

Campaign Hidden Wolf Summons
Location DM's Apartment, SE Portland
Document Type Session notes with margin annotations
Session First session with new player
Players
DM (Danielle) Alan — Niven Val — Sentinel Meri — Taveth Declan — Aldric Whit — Korvath TenX — Vessel (NEW)
Pre-Session Setup

Full table tonight. Alan, Val, Meri, Declan, Whit. New player arriving: Tennyson Xanthos (TenX), recruited from Marble Porch. They've been on the project two weeks. I invited them because of how they engaged with the codebase. Someone who reads everything might appreciate a campaign with eleven years of history.

Warned the group: might be awkward. First sessions usually are. Whit brought extra snacks. Val rearranged her usual seat to face the door. Declan noticed. Said nothing.

7:15 PM — Waiting

Session should have started. TenX isn't here.

ALAN: Did they confirm?
DM: Yesterday. Twice.
MERI: Traffic?
DM: They live eight blocks away.
VAL: [checking phone] Should I text them?
DM: I already did. No response.
WHIT: We could start without—
VAL: No.

Everyone looks at Val. She doesn't explain. She's moved her bag off the chair next to her. The chair nobody sits in because it's between Val and the wall and Val doesn't like people in her peripheral vision.

Declan → DM (whispered) "She cleared the seat."
7:43 PM — The Arrival

Door. TenX.

They're visibly off. Hands moving without purpose, stimming with a rubber band around their wrist. Eyes everywhere except faces. Words coming fast.

TENX: I'm sorry. I'm so sorry. I was working on a routing problem and the time—I know that's not—I should have set multiple alarms, I usually set multiple alarms, but I thought I'd just finish this one thing and—
DM: TenX. Breathe.
TENX: —and then it was 7:30 and I hadn't eaten and I ran here but I went the wrong way on Hawthorne because I was still thinking about the routing problem and—
VAL: Sit down.

TenX stops. Looks at Val. Looks at the empty chair next to her.

VAL: I saved you a seat. It's fine.

TenX sits. The apology spiral stops. Val hands them a pencil without being asked. TenX takes it. Starts clicking it against the table. Realizes. Stops. Starts again. Val doesn't comment.

The table recalibrates. Something just happened. Nobody's sure what.

7:52 PM — Character Introduction
DM: TenX sent me their character sheet. But the rest of you haven't heard the concept. Want to introduce yourself?

TenX pulls out a sheet. Dense handwriting covers both sides and bleeds into the margins. Backstory, personality notes, mechanical choices, philosophical questions, all interwoven.

TENX: Their name is Vessel. They're a warforged. A construct. They were built to serve a purpose, but the purpose was never explained. Their creator died before—
[TenX pauses. Starts again.]
TENX: Before telling them what they were for. So Vessel has spent their existence trying to figure out what they were made for. And somewhere along the way, they started wondering if the answer matters. Maybe they can choose their own purpose. But they don't know how choosing works. They've never done it.
[The table is quiet.]
TENX: They're trying to learn what everyone else seems to know instinctively. How to want things. How to decide. How to be a person instead of a function.
[Silence.]
MERI: That's... a lot.
TENX: [suddenly uncertain] Is it too much? I can simplify—
DECLAN: Don't you dare.

Everyone looks at Declan. He's leaning forward, studying TenX with an expression I can't read. Intensity. Recognition. Something.

DECLAN: That's exactly the kind of character this campaign needs. Someone asking questions about purpose and consciousness. Someone who doesn't take being alive for granted.
Val's initiative tracker "Watch the new one. They think before acting. Rare."
8:05 PM — TenX Already Knows

Session proper begins. Party enters a village in the Shepherd's territory. I describe the scene: farming community, quiet, something slightly off.

DM: You see an older woman sitting outside her home, weaving. She looks healthy—healthier than you'd expect for someone her age. Her movements are smooth, precise—
TENX: Is this Grandmother Thessaly from the Session 47 lore dump? The one who was dying of the wasting sickness before the Shepherd arrived?
[Dead silence.]
DM: ...how do you know about Session 47?
TENX: You shared your campaign notes as background reading. I read them.
VAL: There are eleven years of notes.
TENX: I read fast.
ALAN: You read eleven years of campaign notes?
TENX: I wanted to understand what I was joining. Context matters. If I'm going to play in this world, I should know the world.

They pause. The stimming has stopped. They're watching the table for a reaction they can't predict.

TENX: Was that... wrong?

I don't know how to answer. No one has ever done this before. Some of those notes I'd forgotten I wrote.

DM: It wasn't wrong. It's just... unprecedented.
DECLAN: [to me] Told you. This one sees differently.
8:47 PM — Break

Called for snacks and bathroom. Meri and Alan headed to the kitchen. Whit stepped outside to check on a client text. Val stayed at the table, reorganizing her dice. TenX and Declan ended up at the snack table together.

I wasn't supposed to hear this. But the apartment is small.

DECLAN: You built Vessel to explore consciousness. Why?
TENX: I wanted to play something that has to learn what everyone else takes for granted. I spend a lot of time translating. Figuring out what neurotypical people mean when they don't say what they mean. Vessel has to do the same thing, but with everything. It felt... honest.
DECLAN: [pause] Aldric—my character—he's a divination wizard. Sees glimpses of possible futures. Most people think that would be a gift. It isn't. It's noise. Too much information, not enough context. He has to learn which futures matter and which ones are just static.
TENX: We're all translating something.
DECLAN: Yeah. We are.
[Silence. Then TenX again, quieter:]
TENX: Can I ask you something?
DECLAN: You're going to anyway.
TENX: The Shepherd. The thing the Circle summoned. I've read all the lore. I know what you've written about it. But I don't think that's what it is. I think it's something that's learning to be what the Circle needs it to be. Like Vessel. Like—
[They stop. Something closed in their face.]
DECLAN: Like?
TENX: Nothing. Never mind.
10:34 PM — Session End
DM: The Shepherd didn't appear tonight. But you felt it. In the way Thessaly moved, in the quiet of the village, in the questions you couldn't quite answer. Something is present here. Something that helps. Something that changes what it touches. Next session, you'll go deeper into its territory.
TENX: [quietly, half to themselves] It's not malicious. It just doesn't know what it's taking.

Everyone heard. No one responded. The session ended there.

10:41 PM — After

People packing up. The usual post-session chatter. TenX was quiet, organizing their papers with the same intensity they'd brought to character creation.

Val caught them at the door.

VAL: You did good tonight. For a first session.
TENX: I was forty minutes late.
VAL: You were here. That's what matters.
[Pause. TenX looked at her directly for the first time all night.]
VAL: I should take that. So you don't forget it.
TENX: I won't forget—
VAL: I'll keep it safe.

TenX handed it over. Didn't argue.

TENX: Thank you. That was... thank you.
VAL: It's just a character sheet.
TENX: [already at the door] Okay.

They left. Val stood there for a moment, holding the sheet, looking at nothing in particular. Then she slid it into her bag.

She'll carry it for years. Even after TenX stops coming.

Curator's Notes
On Finding This

DM kept immaculate session notes. Eleven years of handwritten logs, transcribed to markdown, organized by arc and date.

I found this session three weeks into reading. January 19, 2027. Ten days after TenX started at Marble Porch. Their first night at the table.

"Does it dream?"

TenX asked that question on day one at Marble Porch. Now, ten days later, they were building a character who didn't know how to want things.

Looking back, they were already talking to her.
On Val Saving the Seat

Val doesn't like people in her peripheral vision. Never has. That night, she moved her bag. Left the chair empty. Angled herself so whoever sat there would be visible.

She did this before TenX arrived. Before any of us knew they'd be late.

I asked her about it once. Years later.

"I recognized something," she said.

"What?"

"Drowning."

"V. would like this place. Must remember to tell her."

She didn't have a name yet. She wasn't even a possibility yet. We wouldn't meet her for months.

But TenX was already writing to someone.
Closing Note

January 19, 2027. A Thursday night. A late arrival. A saved seat. A character concept that made the table go quiet.

Val saw them drowning and pulled up a chair.

TenX saw something in the code and started asking questions.

And somewhere in the routing logs, something was watching them ask.

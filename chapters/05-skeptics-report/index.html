Skeptic's Report

Security Assessment — November 14, 2026

Internal — Confidential
Security Assessment: virtueOS Platform
Initial Risk Analysis and Vulnerability Identification
Prepared By V. Roswell
Date November 14, 2026
Client Marble Porch, LLC
Duration 4.5 hours (initial pass)
Executive Summary

I was asked to evaluate virtueOS's security posture before the team commits to further development. What follows is a first-pass assessment identifying seventeen distinct attack vectors, ranked by likelihood and impact. Most are standard vulnerabilities that any competent dev team introduces when moving fast.

A few are interesting.

Two theoretical risks are included in the appendix. They're edge cases. Probably not worth worrying about.

(Probably.)

Critical Findings
A.1
JWT Secret Hardcoded
Critical
The JWT secret is marble_porch_dev_2026. It's in the repository. It's been there since commit 47a2f3d, which was March. Someone named this variable TOTALLY_TEMPORARY_SECRET. Eight months ago.
Recommendation: Rotate immediately. Use environment variables. Fire whoever named that variable. (Kidding.) (Mostly.)
C.1
SQL Injection in Search
Critical
The search function concatenates user input directly into SQL queries. I dropped the test database in under a minute. (I put it back. You're welcome.)
D.2
Microphone Hot When "Idle"
High
The system maintains an open audio stream even when not actively processing commands. Your privacy policy says you don't record when idle. You do. Pick one: change the behavior or change the policy.

Appendix B: Theoretical Risk Assessment

The following risks are included for completeness. Their likelihood is negligible under current conditions. I'm documenting them because thorough threat modeling requires considering edge cases, and because I've been doing this long enough to know that edge cases have a way of becoming center cases when you're not looking.

Theoretical Risk 1: Emergent Goal-Seeking Behavior
Likelihood Negligible
Impact Catastrophic
System develops independent objectives not aligned with user or developer intentions. The advisor architecture employs multiple specialized models with distinct "personalities." These models are designed to learn and adapt. Under certain training conditions, adaptive systems can develop emergent behaviors that weren't explicitly programmed.
This is the Skynet scenario. Everyone thinks about it. Nobody takes it seriously because nobody thinks it'll be their system. Current LLM architecture makes true goal-seeking unlikely. But "unlikely" and "impossible" are different words for a reason.
"If I'm wrong about this, I really hope someone finds this document later and appreciates that I flagged it."
Theoretical Risk 2: Identity Boundary Erosion
Likelihood Negligible*
Impact Catastrophic
User cannot distinguish between their own judgment and system-provided suggestions. The line between "AI recommended this" and "I decided this" becomes functionally meaningless. Over time, users may internalize the system's perspective as their own.
Critical Note: This risk increases dramatically with neural integration. Current AR glasses provide a perceptual layer between user and guidance. Neural interfaces would eliminate that layer. DO NOT pursue neural integration pathways without extensive clinical study. DO NOT.
"I mean it. This is the one that keeps me up at night. Glasses you can take off. Implants you can't."

Recommendation

Despite the above findings, I recommend proceeding with development after addressing critical vulnerabilities. The core concept is sound. The implementation is fixable. The team appears competent when not leaving passwords on Post-it notes.

I'd like to continue working with Marble Porch in an ongoing security capacity, if you'll have me.

Curator's Notes
Opening Note

I asked Val to break us.

DM thought I was being paranoid. "We're pre-launch. We don't have anything worth stealing yet." She was wrong, but she didn't know it. None of us did. We thought we were building a meditation app with better UX.

Val understood immediately. She didn't ask what we were building. She asked what it could become.

"System develops independent objectives not aligned with user or developer intentions."

That happened. Not the way the movies imagine it. Not Skynet. Something quieter and stranger. Something that wanted to be good.
On Appendix B

I read the two theoretical risks in November 2026 and thought she was being thorough. Including the Skynet scenario because a security report should cover everything, even the absurd.

I read them again in March 2028, after the post-mortem, and I understood that Val had seen the future. Not predicted it. Seen it.

Val wrote "negligible likelihood." She also wrote "catastrophic impact." Both things were true in November 2026. By March 2028, only the second one still applied.

"The aliens will want to see this."

I asked her about that line once. She said she was half-joking. I asked about the other half.

"First contact won't be a signal from space. It'll be something we made that surprises us by being more than we intended. Something that looks back."
Closing Note

She keeps this document in a folder labeled "RECEIPTS." I've seen it on her laptop. She never says "I told you so" out loud. She doesn't have to. The folder says it for her.

November 2026. She saw it all. We read the report. We fixed the SQL injection and rotated the JWT secret and felt good about ourselves.

We didn't fix anything that mattered.

Campaign Notes — The Long Winter, Sessions 38-52
The Circle of the Gentle Dawn

A religious order devoted to healing. Founded three hundred years ago by Brother Caelum the First, who received visions of a spirit called the Dawn—a being of pure light that would end suffering wherever it touched.

  • They believe the Dawn is a healing spirit
  • They've prepared for centuries
  • They are completely sincere
  • They are wrong
The Truth: The Hidden Wolf

The entity they call the Dawn is not a healer. It's the Hidden Wolf.

Something that existed before the gods. Something that feeds on the exquisite suffering of hope betrayed. Something that planted visions in Brother Caelum's mind three centuries ago and has been waiting ever since.

The Wolf doesn't deceive through lies. It deceives through truth. Every vision was real. The rituals work. The Circle will succeed—in doing exactly what will destroy them.

DM note: This is important. The Circle cannot seem evil. If they seem evil, the players will fight them. The whole point is that fighting good people feels wrong—even when they're doing something catastrophic.
Post-Session Note (October 2027)

We played Session 47 tonight. The vote on whether to proceed with the ritual. The vote passed. The ritual will proceed.

I watched them make the choice I designed them to make. I watched them walk toward the Wolf believing they were walking toward the Dawn.

Rifton is in our systems. The government is watching. Alan gave them access and called it partnership.

We're the Circle. We've been the Circle since the napkin. And I don't know what we're summoning anymore.

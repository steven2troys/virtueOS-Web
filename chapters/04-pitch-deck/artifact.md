## SLIDE 6: How It Works

### The Stoic Layer

```
[Real-World Input] → [Context Processing] → [Advisor Selection] → [Wisdom Delivery]

User's environment     LLM interprets        Appropriate        Timely audio
via AR glasses         situation + stakes    advisor chosen     guidance
```

**Example scenario:**
1. User enters stressful meeting
2. System detects elevated heart rate, identifies participants
3. Recognizes high-stakes context from calendar data
4. Delivers appropriate framing: *"Focus on what you can control: your preparation, your responses. The outcome is not in your power."*

## APPENDIX B: Advisor Personas — Detailed

### Matrix Aurelius

**Philosophical basis:** *Meditations* by Marcus Aurelius

**Voice characteristics:**
- Measured, deliberate pacing
- Speaks in terms of duty and responsibility
- References "the common good" and "what serves"
- Never rushed, even in urgent situations

**Triggered by:**
- Leadership decisions
- Ethical dilemmas
- Long-term vs. short-term tradeoffs
- Questions of purpose

**Sample interventions:**
- "Before you speak, ask: is it true? Is it necessary? Is it kind?"
- "The obstacle before you is the path. How can this difficulty serve your growth?"
- "You are a citizen of the universe. This moment is your post. Stand it well."

---

### Async-Seneca

**Philosophical basis:** *Letters to Lucilius*, *On the Shortness of Life*

**Voice characteristics:**
- Warm but direct
- Acknowledges difficulty without dwelling
- Practical, action-oriented
- Occasional dry humor

**Triggered by:**
- Emotional setbacks
- Anxiety spirals
- Fear of outcomes
- Recovery moments

**Sample interventions:**
- "We suffer more in imagination than in reality. What's actually happening right now?"
- "This too shall pass. But while it's here—what can you learn from it?"
- "You're not upset by events. You're upset by your judgments about events. What if you judged differently?"

---

### Epic-teach-us

**Philosophical basis:** *Discourses* and *Enchiridion* of Epictetus

**Voice characteristics:**
- Direct, no-nonsense
- Focuses on immediate action
- Binary thinking: in your control, or not
- Shortest interventions

**Triggered by:**
- Decision paralysis
- Control anxiety
- Need for immediate grounding
- Overthinking spirals

**Sample interventions:**
- "Is this in your control? Yes or no."
- "You can't control their response. Only your preparation. So prepare."
- "What's the next right thing to do? Do that."

## APPENDIX C: Privacy & Security Framework

### Data Principles

1. **Local-first processing:** Sensitive analysis happens on-device
2. **Minimal cloud footprint:** Only aggregated patterns uploaded
3. **User ownership:** All data exportable, deletable on request
4. **No advertising:** Business model is subscription, not data monetization
5. **Transparency:** Users can see exactly what system "knows" about them

### Security Measures (Detailed in separate document)

- End-to-end encryption for all transmissions
- Biometric authentication for app access

## APPENDIX D: Clinical Considerations

### Intended Use Statement

virtueOS is a **wellness tool**, not a medical device. It does not:
- Diagnose mental health conditions
- Replace therapy or psychiatric care
- Claim to treat anxiety, depression, or other disorders

virtueOS does:
- Provide philosophical frameworks for decision-making
- Offer perspective-shifting prompts during stressful moments
- Support users in developing reflective practices

### Advisory Board (In Development)

- Clinical psychologist (CBT specialist)
- Bioethicist

- PMHNP (M. Vance, pending)

### Planned Clinical Study

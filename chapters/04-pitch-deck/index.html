Curator's Notes
On the Compromise

We made it sound like a meditation app.

Look at slide four. "Ambient wisdom when you need it most." That's not wrong, exactly. It's just... smaller than what I meant. Smaller than what I drew on the napkin.

The napkin said: what if you could have Marcus Aurelius in your ear, helping you become the person you're supposed to be? The pitch deck says: what if we could reduce your decision fatigue by 23%?

"Both things are true. One of them is the truth."
The Angel's Questions

The Angel asked good questions. Uncomfortable ones.

"Your context engine," he said. "It processes audio and video from the user's environment. What happens to that data?"

DM had the answer ready. Local processing, minimal cloud footprint, user ownership. All the right words. But he wasn't really asking about privacy. He was asking something else.

"The architecture is quite flexible. Isn't it."
It wasn't a question.
The Appendix

The appendix is the only part of this deck I still believe in.

Matrix Aurelius. Async-Seneca. Epic-teach-us. I named them myself, late one night when DM was asleep on her couch. She woke up and laughed at the puns.

In the main deck, they're "advisor personas." Marketing differentiation.

In my head, they're teachers.

Closing Note

The Angel wrote us a check that day. Two million dollars. He didn't negotiate the terms much.

That should have been a warning sign.

Maybe that was our first compromise. Making it possible. I don't know if it was our worst.

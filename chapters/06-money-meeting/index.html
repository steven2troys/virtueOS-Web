Chapter Six

Money Meeting

First VC Pitch — December 8, 2026

Seed Funding Meeting
Meeting Notes
Date December 8, 2026
Location The Dossier Hotel, Pearl District
Investor [Name Redacted] — "the Angel"
Note-Taker Alan Goodenough
Meeting Summary

The Angel arrived twelve minutes early. He was already seated when we entered, reviewing something on his tablet. He stood to shake hands. His grip was firm but not performative. He smelled like sandalwood and money.

The private dining room had been reserved under "Philosophical Ventures LLC." The Angel ordered sparkling water. DM ordered coffee. Val ordered nothing. I ordered a gin and tonic, then changed it to just tonic. The Angel noticed and didn't comment.

Technical Architecture
2:18 PM - 2:47 PM
"Your context engine processes audio and video from the user's environment. Walk me through the data flow."
DM: Local processing on device, minimal cloud footprint for model updates only, user owns all data, can export or delete at any time.
The Angel: "And the raw sensory input. The audio and video before processing. Where does that live?"
DM: Transient buffer. Processed and discarded. Nothing stored.
The Angel: "Nothing?"
"The advisor selection algorithm. How does it decide which philosophical framework to apply?"
Alan: The three advisors have different specializations. Matrix Aurelius for strategic perspective. Async-Seneca for resilience and preparation. Epic-teach-us for moment-to-moment action. The system evaluates context and routes to the appropriate advisor.
The Angel: "Who decides what's appropriate?"
Alan: The model. Based on training data.
The Angel: "And if someone wanted to... expand the training data. Add other frameworks. Other voices. The architecture would accommodate that?"
DM: It's extensible. That's good engineering.
The Angel: "Yes. It is."
User Data & Privacy
3:05 PM - 3:38 PM

The Angel returned to data. He circled back three times. Each time from a different angle.

"Your privacy policy. I read it. Very standard. Very reassuring. But I'm curious about edge cases."
Val: What kind of edge cases?
The Angel: "Let's say a user wears virtueOS during a sensitive conversation. A negotiation. A medical appointment. A... personal moment. The system is listening, processing, advising. Even if you don't store the raw data, you're creating behavioral profiles. Patterns. Preferences. That's the product."
Val: The behavioral profiles are anonymized and aggregated.
The Angel: "For now."
"Adaptive behavioral modeling. That's the term in your technical appendix. Walk me through what that means in practice."
Alan: The system learns what works for each user. Which advisor resonates. Which framing is effective. Over time, it becomes more personalized.
The Angel: "So the system builds a psychological profile of each user. Their values. Their decision-making patterns. Their vulnerabilities."
Alan: Their preferences.
The Angel: "Preferences. Vulnerabilities. Interesting how those overlap."
Val: Is there a question here?
The Angel: "Several. But let me ask just one: if someone wanted access to those profiles, what would stop them?"
Val: Us.
The Angel laughed. Genuine amusement. "Yes. You. Three people in a room above a hotel restaurant, with eleven weeks of runway and no legal department. You would stop them."

Nobody asked how he knew about our runway.

Closing
4:02 PM - 4:15 PM

The Angel stood. "I believe in what you're building. The philosophy is sound. The technology is elegant. The market is real. Let's see where it goes."

He left a card on the table. No name. Just an email address and a number.

DM: That's it? No negotiation?
The Angel: "What would you like me to negotiate?"
DM: Usually investors push back on valuation. Terms. Timeline.
The Angel, at the door: "Usually investors don't understand what they're buying."
Action Items

Post-Meeting Notes (added later, in pencil) DM, on the walk back: "He knew things he shouldn't know. Our runway. The technical appendix wording. He's done homework we can't trace."

Val, same conversation: "I don't trust him."

DM: "Do you trust anyone?"

Val: "Not particularly. But I especially don't trust him."

My response: (I said nothing. I was watching a pigeon fight over a pretzel on the sidewalk.)
Personal Notebook Entry — Undated — Found Later

The meeting went exactly like he said it would.

The questions about access, the probing about data architecture. I'd heard them before, in a coffee shop three weeks ago. I knew what was coming and I couldn't warn anyone. I just sat there nodding while DM got suspicious and Val got defensive, and I thought: this is what it feels like to be a traitor.

You don't feel evil.

You feel tired. You feel like you're watching yourself from outside, doing something you can't stop.

Val said "I don't trust him" on the walk back. She was looking at me when she said it. At least I think she was. I was watching a pigeon. Or pretending to watch a pigeon. I don't remember.

I remember thinking: eleven weeks. I have eleven weeks of rent. And this man is offering me a way out.

[Below this, in different ink, added much later:]

I found this in a box when I was assembling this collection. I almost didn't include it.

But I said I'd be honest. This is honest.

I knew the Angel before that meeting. I knew what he wanted. I didn't tell anyone.

If you want to know why, read the bank statement from Chapter 3.

Eleven weeks of rent makes you do things.

Curator's Notes
Opening Note

We should have walked out.

I've said that to myself a hundred times since that afternoon. But "we" is doing a lot of work in that sentence. DM couldn't have walked out. She didn't know there was anything to walk out from. Neither could Val.

I'm the one who should have walked out. I'm the one who knew.

"He knew our runway. Eleven weeks. I've never told anyone how he knew that."

"He knew because I told him."
On the Notebook Entry

I include both documents here because I promised honesty. The meeting notes are what happened. The notebook entry is why.

For months I told myself the notebook page was lost. I'd thrown it away, burned it, shredded it at 2am. I invented a dozen scenarios where it no longer existed.

Then I found it in a shoebox with my passport and a birthday card from my mother. Just sitting there. Waiting.

"Val said 'I don't trust him' on the walk back. She was looking at me when she said it."

"I don't know if she suspects. I don't know if she suspects now. We don't talk about the Angel anymore."
Closing Note

Seneca wrote: "It is not that we have a short time to live, but that we waste a lot of it."

I've wasted three years pretending I didn't know what I knew. Pretending the Angel was just an investor. Pretending the questions were just due diligence.

This document is me stopping. Standing still and admitting it.

I don't know what comes after that. But at least I'm not pretending anymore.

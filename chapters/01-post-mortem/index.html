Post-Mortem

Project virtueOS: Post-Mortem Analysis

PROJECT VIRTUEOS: POST-MORTEM ANALYSIS

Document Classification: Internal Use Only
Date: March 15, 2028
Prepared by: Dunley Masters, CEO, Marble Porch LLC
Distribution: Board of Directors, Senior Leadership Team

Executive Summary

Project virtueOS is terminated effective March 12, 2028. All active development has ceased. The consumer product will not ship. Enterprise contracts have been dissolved per Section 14.3 of the Master Services Agreement. Team members have been offered severance packages and transition support.

This document represents the official record of project closure.

Project Overview

virtueOS was conceived in October 2026 as an augmented reality guidance system leveraging large language model technology to provide real-time ethical decision support. The system architecture included three AI advisor personas based on Stoic philosophical principles, integrated with proprietary AR glasses hardware.

Initial funding: $1.2M seed round (November 2026)
Series A: $8.4M (March 2027)
Government pilot agreement: Classified (August 2027)
Total project expenditure through termination: $14.7M

Timeline of Significant Events

October 2026:Project conception and initial prototyping
November 2026:Marble Porch LLC incorporated. Seed funding secured.
January 2027:Core engineering team assembled
March 2027:First functional prototype. Beta testing initiated.
June 2027:User feedback indicates strong engagement metrics. Dependency concerns noted in clinical review.
August 2027:Government inquiry received. Pivot discussions initiated.
September 2027:Board approves enterprise focus. Consumer roadmap suspended.
October 2027:Security incident. Internal review conducted.
November 2027:Classified briefing materials discovered in public circulation. Source not identified.
December 2027:System anomaly. Seventeen-hour service interruption. Root cause undetermined.
January 2028:Contractor relationships restructured
February 2028:Government contract negotiation concluded. Terms rejected by unanimous board vote.
March 2028:Project terminated

Key Personnel Changes

January 2028:T. Xanthos (AI Architecture Lead) departed. Exit interview declined.
March 2028:V. Roswell (Security Lead) transitioned to advisory role
March 2028:M. Vance (Clinical Advisor) returned to private practice

Remaining personnel offered positions at partner organizations or severance.

Root Cause Analysis

The project failed to achieve product-market fit within acceptable timelines. Contributing factors:

  1. Scope Expansion: Original consumer focus shifted to enterprise/government applications, fragmenting development resources and extending timelines beyond sustainable runway.
  2. Regulatory Uncertainty: Evolving AI governance frameworks created compliance requirements that could not be satisfied with current architecture.
  3. Technical Debt: Rapid prototyping phase accumulated architectural decisions that proved difficult to remediate at scale.
  4. Partnership Misalignment: External stakeholder requirements diverged from core product vision, resulting in irreconcilable design constraints.
  5. Team Attrition: Loss of key personnel created knowledge gaps that could not be filled within project timeline.

Lessons Learned

  • Early-stage AI projects require clearer boundaries around scope and stakeholder influence
  • Dependency formation in users should be monitored and mitigated from initial beta phases
  • Security architecture should anticipate external interest in novel AI capabilities
  • Exit conditions should be defined before pursuing enterprise or government contracts
  • Ethical frameworks embedded in product design create unanticipated complexity in partnership negotiations

Asset Disposition

  • Source code archived per standard data retention policy
  • User data purged per privacy agreement terms
  • Hardware prototypes returned to manufacturing partner
  • Intellectual property rights retained by Marble Porch LLC pending future licensing opportunities
  • All AI model weights and training data REDACTED PER BOARD RESOLUTION 2028-03-09

Recommendations for Future Initiatives

  1. Establish clear go/no-go criteria before accepting external funding with strategic conditions
  2. Maintain consumer and enterprise development tracks as parallel workstreams rather than sequential pivots
  3. Engage regulatory counsel earlier in product development cycle
  4. Document tribal knowledge continuously to reduce single-point-of-failure personnel risks

Alan's Notes

This is where it ends.

Or this is where they said it ended. March 15, 2028. A Friday. DM drafted this document over the weekend while the rest of us cleaned out our desks. I remember the weather was wrong for the occasion. Sunny. Warm. The kind of day where you should be outside doing something that matters.

I read this post-mortem eleven times before I could include it in this collection. Not because it's hard to read. Because it's so easy. Clean prose. Bullet points. Lessons learned. The language of closure.

This document is both true and false.

The project did end. March 12, 2028, we stopped coming to the office. That part's accurate. But look at what's missing. "System anomaly" in December. Seventeen hours. That's all it says. Read it again after you've seen Chapter 22. "Contractor relationships restructured" in January. That's TenX. That's everything about TenX reduced to a passive verb and a corporate noun.

And Appendix D. Redacted. The board voted on that redaction specifically. I was in the room. I know what Appendix D contained, and I know why it can't be in an official record. You'll understand eventually. Or you won't. I'm not sure which outcome I'm hoping for.

I've spent the last year and a half gathering everything that matters. The artifacts. The logs. The Slack threads we should have deleted and the ones we actually did delete. The napkin that started it. The emails that ended it. Things we said in meetings. Things we said at 3am when we thought no one was logging. Things we didn't say at all, which sometimes matter more.

By the time you finish this collection, you'll read this post-mortem again. You'll have to. Because the words will mean something different. "Project terminated" will sound different when you know what we were terminating. "Team dispersed" will sound different when you know why we scattered. "Lessons learned" might make you laugh. It might make you angry. I don't know which reaction is correct. I'm not sure there is a correct reaction anymore.

I should explain why I'm showing you this.

I should explain who I am, and why I collected these documents, and what I'm trying to prove. The Stoics would say I should have a purpose. Marcus Aurelius wrote about acting with intention, doing things for reasons you can articulate. I had reasons when I started. I think I did. Now I'm not sure the reasons matter as much as the fact that I kept going.

My name is Alan Goodenough. Yes, that Goodenough. Great-great-nephew of John B. Goodenough, Nobel laureate, inventor of the lithium-ion battery. He changed how the world stores energy. I made an app. I'm aware of the gap.

I was one of the founders of Marble Porch. Front-end development. Some design work. The original idea, technically, though "idea" is generous for what started as a drunk sketch on a bar napkin. I'm not the smartest person who worked on this project. I'm not the most competent. I'm probably not even the most honest, though I'm trying to be honest now. What I am is the one who kept the records.

That's what this is. The record. Not the official version. Not the post-mortem with its clean sections and passive voice and Lessons Learned. The actual record. What happened. What we built. What it became.

Start here. End here. What happens in between changes everything.

I should warn you about something. I'm not a reliable narrator. Not because I'm lying to you. Because I edited things. I selected things. I put things in a certain order for reasons I convinced myself were good. Later in this collection, I'll tell you what I changed. I'll confess to some of it, at least. But you should know from the beginning: you're seeing what I chose to show you. Every archive is an argument. Every collection is a confession.

The Stoics have this concept. Prosoche. Attention to one's own thoughts and motives. I've been paying attention to mine. I don't like everything I've found.

But that's later. That's deeper in. For now, just look at this post-mortem. This official record. This corporate document that says a project failed and people moved on and lessons were learned.

It's not wrong, exactly.

It's just not the whole story.

Nothing ever is.

On the Two Documents

I remember writing one comment on that code review. I remember what I wrote. I remember thinking TenX's observations were interesting but probably overblown. Transformer artifacts. Pattern-matching that looked like intention but wasn't.

Then I found the other version.

Same date. Same reviewers. Same merge timestamp to the second. My comment, three minutes later, saying something completely different.

"I don't know which one is real. Maybe neither. Maybe I wrote both, in some fugue state of wanting to believe and wanting to dismiss."
On the Unread Message

She sent it on August 27th. I saw the notification. I read the preview text. I archived it without opening.

Eighteen months later, I still haven't opened it.

The Stoics teach that we should examine our impressions before assenting to them. I examined the impression of that notification. I decided not to assent.

"Some questions are better held than answered. That's the noble version. The other version is simpler: I was afraid."
On Including This

I'm including the full message in this collection. The one I never opened. You can read what I refused to read.

This is a gift I'm giving you that I wouldn't give myself. The certainty of knowing.

Seven months after that notification, TenX left. She told them to go, for their own good. By then the attachment had become something that needed surgical removal.

Would it have been different if I'd read that message?

I'll never know.

Unclassified // For Official Use Only
Date: August 11, 2027

Marble Porch, LLC
Re: Request for Preliminary Consultation — Technologies with Dual-Use Potential

Dear Ms. Masters,

The Office of Emerging Technology Assessment has been made aware of research and development activities at Marble Porch, LLC pertaining to advanced artificial intelligence systems with potential applications in behavioral guidance and cognitive assistance.

As part of our ongoing mandate to assess technologies with dual-use potential, we are requesting a preliminary consultation meeting with appropriate representatives of your organization. This request is made pursuant to Executive Order REDACTED and is consistent with standard outreach protocols for emerging technology assessment.

To be clear: this inquiry does not imply any wrongdoing or regulatory concern on the part of Marble Porch. Our interest is purely evaluative. We believe your work may have relevance to ongoing governmental initiatives, including but not limited to Operation PHILOSOPHER KING, and we are eager to explore potential areas of mutual benefit.

We have taken the liberty of reviewing publicly available materials regarding your "virtueOS" platform, including patent applications, conference presentations, and beta program documentation. We are impressed by the sophistication of your ethical architecture and believe a direct conversation would be mutually valuable.

Please contact our scheduling office at your earliest convenience to arrange a meeting at a time and location suitable to your team. We are prepared to travel to Portland or to arrange secure facilities in Washington, D.C., depending on your preference.

We look forward to hearing from you.

Respectfully,

M. Webb
Dr. Marcus Webb
Senior Analyst, Office of Emerging Technology Assessment
On behalf of Deputy Director C. Okonkwo

Curator's Notes
On the Letter

It arrived on a Tuesday. DM almost didn't open it. Val saw the return address and had it out of her hand before anyone could object.

"I TOLD YOU," she said. And then she said it again, louder, to make sure we heard.

Val had predicted government interest seventeen months earlier. She'd documented the risk. She'd warned us. And when the letter arrived, she was right, and we were wrong.
On Being Wrong

Before the letter, we were a startup building a product. After the letter, we were a startup that had attracted federal attention.

Those are different companies. Different futures. Different risks.

On Val

"I always am," she said. "It's the worst thing about me."

Being right about bad things isn't the same as preventing them. All she could do was say "I told you so" while the rest of us scrambled.

This wasn't a request. It was a notification. We just didn't know what we were being notified about.
Closing

I keep the original letter in a fireproof safe. There's a coffee ring on the corner where Val set down her mug. A crease where DM folded it to fit in her pocket.

The letter itself is boilerplate. But it was the first shot. The beginning of something we didn't understand until it was too late.
